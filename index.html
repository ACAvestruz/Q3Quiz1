FILM REVIEWS


Monster (2023)

TRAILER

SYNOPSIS:

The story unfolds with three perspective: the mother, the teacher, and the child. The story starts with Saori noticing her son’s destructive behavior and when she “finds out” something is wrong, she storms into the school demanding to know what happened. The teacher, Hori, who she deemed responsible actually misinterpreted the protectiveness of Minato as bullying to his classmate, Yori. In the final perspective, we see the story unfold through Minato’s eyes — an innocent friendship that may be something more. [IMDB]

CAST AND CREW:

Directed by: Hirokazu Kore-eda

Written by: Yuji Sakamoto

Cast: Sakura Ando, Eita Nagayama, Soya Kurokawa, Hinata Hiiragi [Wikipedia]

REVIEW:

⭐ 5 /5

PLOT | 5/5
It beautiful queer film that has an open ending. The film has three perspectives and after watching the last perspective which is Minato’s (Soya Kurokawa), all the pieces suddenly line up.

VISUALS | 5/5
As seen, it has very beautiful visuals and it has a lot of visual metaphors and easter eggs.

CHARACTERS | 5/5
All of the characters are very well rounded and I love how someone who seems like a “monster” in another’s perspective is caused because of misunderstandings.

MUSIC | 5/5
The music is by Ryuichi Sakamoto (who died after writing the film’s music) really enhanced the film. Other than the music, the small audio easter eggs are very rewarding when you catch it.


The Wind Rises (2013)


TRAILER

SYNOPSIS:

Jiro Horikoshi dreams of flying airplanes because of the Italian aeronautical designer Caproni. Unfortunately, he has bad eyesight so he becomes an aeronautical engineer. He joins a major Japanese engineering company in 1927 and becomes one of the world's most innovative and accomplished airplane designers. The film portrays important historical events before and during the World War 2. Even being a busy individual, he falls in love with the beautiful and soft-natured artist Nahoko and grows a close friendship with his colleague Honjo. [IMDB]

CAST AND CREW:

Directed by: Hayao Miyazaki

Written by: Hayao Miyazaki

Cast: Hideaki Anno, Miori Takimoto, Hidetoshi Nishijima, Masahiko Nishimura [Wikipedia]

REVIEW:

⭐ 4.5 /5

PLOT | 4/5
Unlike other Ghibli films I have watched, The Wind Rises didn’t have magical and other-worldly sequences and is very grounded and realistic. The film is based on the book “The Wind Has Risen”. The difference of the Ghibli movie to the book is that in the book, the main character is unnamed and in the film, the main character is based off Jiro Horikoshi.

VISUALS | 5/5
With Ghibli style, it enhanced Jiro’s passion of airplanes and his love story with Nahoko.

CHARACTERS | 5/5
The plot is quite controversial but the characters really made up for it. It is realistic and so heartfelt.

MUSIC | 4/5
It is not quite as memorable as Howl’s Moving Castle, Spirited Away, or Ponyo but it really enhanced the scenes.


Mula sa Buwan (Proshot) (2023)

TRAILER

SYNOPSIS:

Mula sa Buwan is based on the play Cyrano de Bergerac by Edmond Rostand. This musical doesn’t have your usual Cyrano — it still has the story of requited love, beauty, and battles but the story is reimagined in 1940’s Manila with stories about wide-eyed dreamers, fools, and misfits. With haranas, kundimans, wit, stories, and music, Mula sa Buwan weaves stories about young Filipinos during the 40’s. When the World War 2 strikes, the young protagonists of the story are suddenly forced to “grow up”. In the center of all of this is the bittersweet love story between the poet Cyrano and his childhood friend Roxanne and the handsome and kind-hearted Christian. [Official Website]

CAST AND CREW:

Directed by: Pat Valera

Adapted by: Pat Valera

Original Lyrics by:Pat Valera, William Elvin Manzano

Cast: Myke Salomon, Gab Pangilinan, Markki Stroem, Phi Palmos, MC Dela Cruz, Jon Abella, Jillian Ita-as [Official Website]

REVIEW:

⭐ 4.75 /5

PLOT | 5/5
I have previously watched the musical live last December 2022 so I already expected everything. The plot is based on Cyrano De Bergerac and due to this musical, I am able to discover the original play. I really love the plot of Mula Sa Buwan; ranging from unrequited love to finding solace within other misfits (with Rosanna’s theater troupe).

VISUALS | 4.5/5
Unfortunately, like most proshots of musicals, the filmed versions doesn’t really capture the beauty of live theater. For example, it was very obvious that Cyrano's nose is badly put as a prosthetic.

CHARACTERS | 5/5
It is an adjustment for me seeing Markki Stroem as Christian (the one I was able to watch live is MC Dela Cruz) and MC Dela Cruz as Maximo (the one I watched December 2023 was Cedrick Juan). As usual, Gab Pangilinan and Myke Salomon’s chemistry shines in their scenes.

MUSIC | 5/5
The music of Pat Valera and William Elvin Manzano is a beautiful combination of upbeat theatrical sarswela-rock and lyrics in deep Filipino. The sound is super rich on live theater that I think that the audio mixing for the proshot is very hard to do.
